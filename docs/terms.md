On-Air Sponsorship, Underwriting, and PSA Guidelines

Adherence to these On-Air Underwriting Guidelines will ensure compliance with the station's obligations under its FCC license and station policies.

1. Underwriting acknowledgments are generally expected to last 15 -20 seconds and will not exceed 30 seconds.

2. Underwriting acknowledgments will be played between programs or other breaks so they do not interrupt regular programming.

3. Underwriting acknowledgments will be recorded and produced as a dry read or with non-promotional music or the spot may be played during a music bed between programs.

4. For underwriting by non-profit organizations, the organization’s nonprofit status will be mentioned in the acknowledgment.

5. Underwriting acknowledgments MAY NOT Contain the following language:
    * Language that is promotional
    * Comparative or qualitative language to describe an event, product, or service. For example, do not use words such as “good,” “great,” “best,” "better," “highly regarded,” “famous,” “award winning,” “latest,” “featuring”, “starring” (“presents” is ok), etc.
    * Repetition of information
    * Any reference or allusion to cost (including “free”), discounts, or sales
    * Calls to action (directly telling the listener to do something)
    * Inducements to buy, sell, or lease (e.g., buy-one; get-one)

6. Underwriting acknowledgments MAY contain the following language:
    * Name of Underwriter
    * Underwriter's address, web site, and phone number
    * Length of time underwriter has been in business
    * Brand or trade name of the underwriter's product, service, or event
    * Name of Underwriter's product, service, or event
    * Underwriter's well-established slogan that the public already associates with the Underwriter
    * Value-neutral description of an event with a date, service, or product.

7. Your announcements are in rotation with announcements from other station supporters.

8. The station reserves the right to not accept underwriting support from any entity deemed to be inconsistent with the station's mission, not in the general interest of its listeners, or potentially detrimental to the welfare and image of the station.
